CASE FILE: PSC-2026-INKWELL-01

The Grand Inkwell Mystery

Investigation Active — Last Update: ████████████

Case Summary

Approximately ██ months ago, PSC experienced an unprecedented event. A pen of legendary significance—the codename for which is ██████████— vanished from the high-security vault maintained by SoSAS.

The pen was never logged as missing. No alarms triggered. No records were altered. Yet somehow, someone knew exactly where it was, when to retrieve it, and how to circumvent every failsafe in the PSC security apparatus.

The Grand Inkwell was not stolen. It was recovered. But recovered by whom? And why?

Key Evidence

Evidence A: A single strand of ███████ fiber found at the breach point. Origin unknown. Material composition suggests ██████████████.
Evidence B: A note left in the vault, written in fountain pen, containing only the words: "The writer returns home."
Evidence C: Security footage from the night in question shows a figure in a ████████ approaching the vault. The footage corrupts for exactly 47 seconds at the critical moment.
Evidence D: An ink analysis performed on the note reveals traces of a formula last used in ████████.

Timeline of Events

  • Day -1: SoARC completes inventory audit. All pens accounted for.
  • Night 0: Security anomaly detected at 02:47 AM. ████████ bypassed.
  • Day 1: Audit performed. One pen missing. SoSAS director notified. Investigation classified.
  • Day 3: Analysis of evidence begins. Fountain pen identified as ████████ era.
  • Day 7: Theories conflict. SoARC claims the pen was misclassified from the start.
  • Day 14: Lead investigator placed on leave. Investigation continues under seal.
  • Now: Case remains open. The Grand Inkwell awaits discovery.

Prime Suspects & Theories

Theory A: The Archivist's Return
An SoARC member, ████████, has been flagged as having knowledge of the vault's location. Motive unknown. Last known location: ██████████.
Theory B: The Inside Job
Analysis suggests the thief had intimate knowledge of PSC security protocols. Could be current staff. Could be former staff. Could be someone with access we don't even know about.
Theory C: The Pen Chose Its Owner
A minority theory, but one worth noting: pens of legendary status are said to call to their true keepers. What if The Grand Inkwell wasn't stolen—but simply answered when summoned?

Investigation Notes (Excerpt)

The Grand Inkwell represents more than just a pen. Its mechanics are centuries ahead of its time. Its ink composition is ████████████. Its provenance traces back to ██████, suggesting involvement of ██████████████.

One investigator theorized: "If this pen was stolen, why hasn't it surfaced on the black market? If it was recovered, why hasn't it been returned? Unless... it's already been used. Unless someone is writing something important with it right now."

The truth is hidden in the very thing that brought us here: the written word. Somewhere in PSC's archives, in someone's journal, or in the margins of an old catalog, the Grand Inkwell's story continues to be written—by the pen itself, through its owner.
